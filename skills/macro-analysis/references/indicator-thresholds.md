## Yield Curve

### 10Y-3M Spread
- **Best recession predictor**: inverted before every US recession since 1969
- Average lead time before recession: ~11 months
- Inversions lasting 3+ months: ~73% predict recession (vs 45% for shorter)
- ⚠️ Critical: the RE-STEEPENING after prolonged inversion often coincides with recession onset

### 10Y-2Y Spread
- Inverted before 7 of last 8 recessions (~87.5% hit rate)
- Lead time: 6–24 months (wide range, poor for precise timing)
- S&P 500 typically peaks 6–9 months after initial inversion (range: 2–15 months)

### Signal Classification
| 10Y-2Y Spread | Signal |
|---------------|--------|
| > +100bp | 🟢 Healthy steep curve |
| +50bp to +100bp | 🟢 Normal |
| 0 to +50bp | 🟡 Flattening — watch closely |
| < 0 (inverted) | 🔴 Recession warning |
| Re-steepening after prolonged inversion | 🔴🔴 Recession may be imminent |

## Credit Spreads

### HY OAS (ICE BofA US High Yield)
| Level | Interpretation |
|-------|---------------|
| < 300bp | Extreme complacency; limited compression upside |
| 300–350bp | 🟢 Tight but normal in expansions |
| 350–500bp | 🟡 Moderate; default risk pricing normal |
| 500–700bp | 🔴 Stress emerging; historically a regime-shift threshold |
| 700–1000bp | 🔴🔴 Serious stress; approaching crisis levels |
| > 1000bp | 🔴🔴🔴 Crisis (every bear market since 2000 saw HY > 1000bp) |

### IG OAS (ICE BofA US Corporate)
| Level | Interpretation |
|-------|---------------|
| < 80bp | Extreme tight |
| 80–130bp | 🟢 Normal expansion |
| 130–200bp | 🟡 Widening, monitor |
| > 200bp | 🔴 Significant stress |

### Key insight
The "excess bond premium" (spread beyond default risk) is one of the strongest single
predictors of future GDP and stock returns at 0–4 quarter horizons.
Credit spread WIDENING typically precedes equity weakness.

## PMI / ISM

### ISM Manufacturing PMI
| Level | Interpretation |
|-------|---------------|
| > 55 | 🟢 Strong expansion |
| 50–55 | 🟢 Moderate expansion |
| 47–50 | 🟡 Manufacturing contracting, but economy may still grow |
| 42.3–47 | 🔴 Significant weakness; GDP growth threatened |
| < 42.3 | 🔴🔴 Historical correlation with GDP contraction |

### What matters most
- **Direction > level**: A PMI rising from 45 to 48 is more bullish than falling from 55 to 52
- **New Orders sub-index**: Leads headline PMI by 1–2 months
- **PMI z-score below -1 SD**: strongly predicts poor equity factor performance
- PMI peaks tend to lead equity peaks by 0–3 months

## Fed Policy Rate

### After first rate CUT in a cycle
- Stocks positive 80% of the time over next 12 months, average +15%
- BUT: when recession accompanies cuts, >50% of cases saw S&P 500 decline -22% to -55%
- **Key question**: Is the Fed cutting for insurance (bullish) or in panic (bearish)?

### After first rate HIKE in a cycle
- Equities return average +6.2% over 12 months (below long-term avg by ~200bp)
- Not catastrophic but below trend

### Rule of thumb
"Don't fight the Fed" — but context matters more than direction.
Insurance cuts in healthy economy → bullish
Panic cuts in recession → initially bearish

## NFCI

### Chicago Fed National Financial Conditions Index
Synthesizes 105 financial indicators. Zero = average conditions.

| Level | Interpretation |
|-------|---------------|
| < -0.7 | 🟢 Very loose financial conditions |
| -0.7 to -0.3 | 🟢 Loose |
| -0.3 to 0 | 🟡 Near average |
| 0 to +0.5 | 🔴 Tighter than average |
| > +1.0 | 🔴🔴 Severe tightening, elevated recession risk |

Leads real economic growth by 1–2 quarters.

## M2 Money Supply

### YoY Growth Rate
| Growth | Interpretation |
|--------|---------------|
| > 10% | Highly expansionary — inflationary pressure building |
| 5–10% | 🟢 Normal expansion |
| 0–5% | 🟡 Modest growth |
| < 0% (contracting) | 🔴 Liquidity withdrawal — headwind for asset prices |

### Key relationship
M2 growth leads PCE inflation by approximately 18 months.
Example: M2 peaked at +26.9% YoY in Feb 2021 → PCE inflation peaked June 2022.
Best used as a background regime indicator, not for short-term equity timing.

## Conference Board LEI

### Six-Month Growth Rate
| Rate | Interpretation |
|------|---------------|
| > 0% | 🟢 Expansion signal |
| -2% to 0% | 🟡 Slowing |
| -4.2% to -2% | 🔴 Elevated recession risk |
| < -4.2% with diffusion < 50 | 🔴🔴 Official recession signal ("3Ds rule") |

Leads business cycle turning points by ~7 months.
Outperforms all other individual indicators at signaling recessions up to 9 months ahead.

## VIX

| Level | Interpretation |
|-------|---------------|
| < 13 | 🟢 Low vol, complacency (can be contrarian warning) |
| 13–16 | 🟢 Normal calm |
| 16–20 | 🟡 Slightly elevated |
| 20–25 | 🟡 Elevated uncertainty |
| 25–30 | 🔴 Fear |
| > 30 | 🔴🔴 Acute fear / panic |
| > 40 | 🔴🔴🔴 Crisis-level (GFC hit 80+, COVID hit 82) |

### VIX term structure
- **Contango** (front < back): Normal, risk-on
- **Backwardation** (front > back): Acute fear, potential crash signal

## USD (DXY / UUP)

Strong USD headwinds:
- S&P 500 multinationals (~40% revenue international): strong USD → earnings drag
- Emerging markets: strong USD → EM equity/debt pressure
- Commodities: strong USD → price pressure

### Trend assessment
Use 20-day MA vs 60-day MA of UUP:
- 20d < 60d (weakening): 🟢 Risk-On for equities
- 20d > 60d (strengthening): 🔴 Risk-Off headwind

## Oil Price

| WTI Level | Interpretation |
|-----------|---------------|
| < $50 | Demand concerns / oversupply |
| $50–80 | 🟢 Healthy range for consumers and producers |
| $80–100 | 🟡 Elevated but manageable |
| > $100 | 🔴 Begins to weigh on consumer spending |
| > $130 | 🔴🔴 Recession risk sharply elevated |

Hamilton's finding: real oil price increases of 50–100% within a year preceded most postwar recessions.

## Copper/Gold Ratio

Historical correlation with 10Y Treasury yield: ~0.85 (has weakened since 2023).

- Rising ratio: 🟢 Expansion signal, risk-on
- Falling ratio: 🔴 Contraction signal, risk-off

### Caveats (post-2023)
The traditional relationship has weakened due to:
- Record central bank gold buying (structural demand unrelated to risk aversion)
- Green transition copper demand (structural demand unrelated to business cycle)
- Fiscal deficit-driven term premium changes
Always note these caveats when interpreting.

## AUD/JPY

Risk appetite proxy used by institutional FX desks:
- AUD = commodity/risk currency; JPY = safe-haven/funding currency
- Rising AUD/JPY: 🟢 Global risk appetite increasing
- Falling AUD/JPY: 🔴 Risk aversion, potential carry trade unwind

### Sharp JPY strengthening
Watch for sudden >2% daily moves — can trigger cascading carry trade unwinds
with global risk-off implications (as seen in Aug 2024 JPY carry unwind).
